About

Hello! I'm a roboticist based in Albuquerque, NM, with long-term interests are in optimal control theory and online learning-based autonomy for physical robotic systems. In May 2026, I received a degree in electrical engineering from the University of New Mexico.

My previous work at the Air Force Research Laboratory focused on integrating Crazyflie micro-UAVs into the LINCS aviary, a motion-capture-based testbed for evaluating spacecraft autonomy algorithms. Using ROS 2 for communication and command handling, and a Vicon motion-capture system for state feedback, I developed software for multi-agent flight experiments, trajectory tracking, simulation-to-real testing, and autonomous behavior validation.

Before that, I worked with MIT's Robust Robotics Group on evaluating BRULE, a belief-space planner for navigation with uncertain and degrading landmarks. I developed deterministic sampling-based planners in a 2D environment with landmark priors, compared final-state position estimates, and later extended the work into ROS 2 and Gazebo using a 3D wheeled agent with cameras and AprilTags as surrogate landmarks.
